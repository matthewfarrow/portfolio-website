01Tropic Vote

The thing I actually run

Live on Base

Tropic Vote Live Base tropic.vote ↗

veHYDX holders delegate earning power, and nothing else. The NFT stays in the wallet it started in, and it stays there the entire time. Every epoch the protocol reads the bribe and fee surface across every gauge, routes that earning power to the pools with the best marginal expected value, votes, and claims. Holders keep 95 percent of what comes back. The fee is the other five.

The hard part was never the optimizer. It was proving the edge was real, epoch by epoch, against the on-chain record, instead of asking anyone to trust a number in a dashboard.

I wrote it, I run it, and I sell it.

01

Delegate earning power. One approval. The token itself is not transferred, and cannot be.

02

Read the surface. Bribes and fees across every gauge, every epoch, before the vote closes.

03

Route by marginal EV. Allocation goes where the next unit of vote is worth the most, not where the headline APR is loudest.

04

Vote and auto-claim. It runs itself weekly. You keep 95 percent and can leave whenever you want.

04Origin

I have been doing a version of this job since I was sixteen, in a high school cafeteria, for free.

I founded the investment club at San Clemente High School and led it for three years. I built an Ethereum mining rig in my bedroom. My friends and I spent lunch periods writing crypto trading bots that were, in hindsight, mostly bad.

At Purdue I co-founded Boiler Blockchain and helped build the university's first undergraduate blockchain course, while managing a small portfolio for friends who trusted me before there was much reason to. Crypto returns came in around 1,900%3.

None of it was a plan. It was the only thing I ever wanted to read about, and it turned out that reading about something for ten years is a qualification.
